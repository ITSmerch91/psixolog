Our Team

DR. TALI SHENFIELD

CLINICAL DIRECTOR

Dr. Tali Shenfield is an expert in the areas of school psychology and child clinical psychology. She holds a PhD in Psychology from the University of Toronto and is licensed as a Psychologist by the College of Psychologists of Ontario. Dr. Shenfield is accredited by the Canadian Register of Health Service Providers in Psychology and is a member of the Canadian Psychological Association. She has several publications in scientific journals and is a frequent presenter at international conferences and symposia. Dr. Shenfield lectured at the University of Toronto and worked as a child psychologist at the Hospital for Sick Children, Toronto District School Board, York Catholic District School Board, and the Hincks-Dellcrest Centre, providing clinical and psychoeducational services to a wide range of clients. She is the Clinical Director of our center and provides supervision and oversight to other psychologists and psychotherapists.

LANGUAGES: ENGLISH AND RUSSIAN

DR. WILLI STEINKE

Dr. Willi Steinke is a licensed school and clinical psychologist and a member of the College of Psychologists of Ontario. He holds a PhD in Psychology from Queen's University and has over 15 years of experience working with children and adolescents in the Toronto District School Board and the Halton District School Board. Dr. Steinke has many publications in various psychology and neuropsychology journals and has presented his research at international conferences and workshops. He taught undergraduate and graduate courses at Queen's University and Southern Methodist University. At our centre, Dr. Steinke supervises psycho-educational assessments and provides therapeutic services for children and adolescents.

Dr. Steinke uses CBT and Strength-Based therapeutic approach to help clients who struggle with attentional, behavioral, self-esteem issues as well as anxiety and depression. His extensive experience at the school board provides him with an unique understanding of how learning obstacles affect children’s social interactions, confidence and behavior.

ANAT ELBAUM, MA

Anat Elbaum is an experienced therapist who has worked in various mental health public and private agencies in Canada and abroad. Ms. Elbaum has 30 years of experience treating men, women, children and adolescents. Her areas of specialization include treating sexual, physical and emotional trauma, separation, divorce and blended families, marital and family conflicts, anxiety, depression and school related challenges. She has helped people who experienced adjustment to major life events like adoption, immigration and grief, difficulty in parent/child relations, parenting challenging children and other issues. Ms. Elbaum is a versatile clinician who draws on several therapeutic approaches such as Family and Marriage Therapy, Attachment Focused Therapy, Cognitive Behavioural Therapy, Trauma Focused Therapy, Short Term Therapy, Play Therapy, Expressive Arts Therapy, Therapeutic Cards. She brings clients to change with respect, warmth, empathy and humour. Ms. Elbaum is a Registered clinical Family and Marriage Therapist (RMFT) and a Registered Psychotherapist (CRPO) licensed to work in the province of Ontario. She was Adjunct Lecturer at The Faculty of Social Work at The University of Toronto.

LANGUAGES: ENGLISH AND HEBREW
